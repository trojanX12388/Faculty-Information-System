Exploring Sustainable Tourism Development: A Case Study of Community-Based Ecotourism in the Philippines

Author: Prof. Maria Santos

Year: 2022-2023

Published
Completed

Maria Santos' master's thesis investigates the dynamics of sustainable tourism development through a case study on community-based ecotourism in the Philippines. This research aims to examine the impacts of community involvement, environmental conservation practices, and economic benefits on the overall sustainability of tourism initiatives within local communities. Utilizing a mixed-methods approach, Maria delves into the perspectives of community members, tourists, and stakeholders involved in the ecotourism project. Through surveys, interviews, and on-site observations, she analyzes the social, economic, and environmental dimensions of the community-based ecotourism model. The findings of the study provide valuable insights into the challenges, successes, and opportunities for promoting sustainable tourism practices at the local level. Maria Santos' master's thesis serves as a significant contribution to the discourse on sustainable tourism in the Philippines, offering practical recommendations for policymakers, community leaders, and tourism practitioners aiming to foster ecotourism initiatives that benefit both the environment and the local communities.

Promoting Mental Health Awareness in Filipino Schools: An Intervention Framework

Author: Prof. Maria Santos

Year: 2022-2023

Published
Completed

Maria Santos' master's thesis focuses on the critical issue of mental health in Filipino schools, presenting an intervention framework to promote awareness and support among students. The research aims to address the growing concerns related to mental health challenges faced by students and advocates for proactive measures within the educational system. Employing a qualitative research design, Maria conducts in-depth interviews with students, teachers, and mental health professionals to understand the prevailing issues and gather insights into effective intervention strategies. The thesis proposes a comprehensive framework that includes awareness campaigns, mental health education, and support systems within the school environment. Through this work, Maria Santos contributes to the ongoing dialogue about mental health in the Philippines, particularly in the context of education. The proposed intervention framework provides a blueprint for schools and educational institutions to create a nurturing environment that prioritizes the mental well-being of students, fostering a culture of understanding and support.
