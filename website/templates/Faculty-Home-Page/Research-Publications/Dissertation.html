Exploring Indigenous Knowledge Systems in Agriculture: A Case Study of Sustainable Farming Practices in Ifugao Province

Author: Juan dela Cruz, Phd

Year: 2022-2023

Published
Completed

Juan dela Cruz's groundbreaking dissertation embarks on an immersive journey into the heart of Ifugao Province, unraveling the intricate tapestry of indigenous knowledge systems in agriculture. With a meticulous blend of ethnography and agricultural science, this study seeks to understand, document, and celebrate the time-honored traditions of Ifugao farmers. Focusing on sustainable farming practices, including the architectural marvel of rice terracing and the centuries-old wisdom embedded in crop rotation, Juan aims to bridge the gap between traditional agricultural wisdom and contemporary sustainability efforts. Through an exhaustive exploration of these practices, the research endeavors not only to contribute to the preservation of cultural heritage but also to offer insights that can catalyze the evolution of environmentally conscious farming practices on a broader scale.

Digital Literacy Among Filipino Youth: Implications for Educational Policies and Practices

Author: Prof. Maria Hernandez, Phd

Year: 2022-2023

Published
Completed

In an era defined by rapid technological advancement, Maria Hernandez's comprehensive dissertation immerses itself in the evolving landscape of digital literacy among Filipino youth. Navigating the uncharted waters of an increasingly digitalized world, this research is a testament to Maria's commitment to understanding and addressing the challenges and opportunities posed by technology integration in education. Through a multifaceted approach that combines educational psychology, policy analysis, and pedagogical theory, Maria aims to not only delineate the current state of digital literacy but also to carve a path forward. By proposing policy implications and innovative educational practices, the study aspires to equip Filipino youth with the necessary digital skills to navigate the complexities of the digital realm with confidence, competence, and ethical acumen.
Community Resilience in the Aftermath of Natural Disasters: A Comparative Analysis of Typhoon Response Strategies in Coastal Barangays

Author: Prof. Andres Reyes, Phd

Year: 2022-2023

Published
Completed

Andres Reyes' dissertation stands as a poignant exploration of the resilience exhibited by coastal communities in the Philippines in the wake of natural disasters, specifically typhoons. Through a meticulous comparative analysis of response strategies in various coastal barangays, the study aims to unearth effective community-based approaches for disaster preparedness, response, and recovery. Andres endeavors to illuminate the intricacies of resilience within the cultural and geographical context of these communities, providing a nuanced understanding of the socio-economic, environmental, and cultural factors that contribute to their ability to withstand and recover from the impacts of natural disasters. By documenting these strategies, the research not only contributes to academic knowledge but also aims to provide valuable insights for policymakers, disaster management professionals, and community leaders working toward building more resilient societies.
